Buyer Questions

Everything an Importer Needs to Know

Every question buyers commonly ask before placing their first order — covering ordering, payment, documentation, compliance, and region-specific requirements. If yours is not here, WhatsApp us directly.

Ordering & Minimum Quantities
What is the minimum order quantity?

Sona Masuri rice: one 20-foot FCL (approximately 24–26 metric tonnes depending on pack size).

Coconut products: LCL orders accepted from 500 kg, making it practical to test the product in your market before scaling up to a full container.

Can I mix products in one container?

Yes. Rice and coconut products can be combined in a single FCL provided storage conditions are compatible. We advise on product compatibility during the quotation process.

Can I get samples before ordering?

Yes. We dispatch internationally compliant sample packs via DHL or FedEx to any country. You pay only the courier cost, which is fully deducted from your first commercial order invoice.

Samples include: product in standard retail packaging, relevant lab report, product specification sheet, and company profile.

How do I get a price quote?

Use the enquiry form on this page or WhatsApp us. To generate an accurate quote, please provide:

  • Product name and quantity required
  • Destination port or country
  • Preferred pack size
  • Preferred Incoterm (FOB, CFR, or CIF)

We respond with a formal pro-forma invoice within one business day.

How often do prices change?

Rice and coconut are commodity-linked and fluctuate with harvests, currency movements, and global demand. Quoted prices are valid for 7 days from the date of pro-forma invoice. We lock in pricing once a purchase order and advance payment are confirmed.

Payment
What payment methods do you accept?

Letter of Credit (LC at Sight) — irrevocable LC from any major internationally recognised bank. The safest option for first-time buyers.

Bank Transfer (TT) — 50% advance at order confirmation, 50% against the Bill of Lading copy before shipment release.

We do not accept PayPal, cryptocurrency, or mobile payment apps for commercial shipments.

What currency do you invoice in?

All invoices are issued in USD by default. EUR and GBP are available on request. INR invoicing is for Indian buyers only.

What is an LC at Sight and how does it work?

An irrevocable Letter of Credit at Sight is a bank payment instrument. Your bank guarantees payment to us once we present the correct shipping documents (BL, invoice, packing list, CoO, phytosanitary certificate). Neither party takes credit risk.

For orders above USD 10,000, we strongly recommend LC at Sight. Your bank’s trade finance desk can open one in 3–5 business days.

Packaging & Labelling
What pack sizes are available?

Rice: 1 kg, 5 kg, 10 kg, 25 kg, 50 kg. Custom sizes available for orders of 5 FCL+ with 3–4 weeks additional lead time.

Coconut products: Jute bags (50 kg), PP woven bags (25–50 kg), corrugated cartons for retail. Specific formats on request.

Do you offer private label / supermarket own-brand packaging?

Yes. Full private label with your brand name, logo, and design. Requirements: confirmed order of at least 3 FCL or equivalent, print-ready artwork, and 3–4 weeks advance notice before the shipment date.

Do you supply Arabic / French / German / other language labels?

Yes. We produce dual-language packaging in English + Arabic, English + French, English + German, English + Spanish, and other combinations on request. Labels include all required fields: product name, ingredients, nutritional information, country of origin, net weight, and importer details block.

What information is printed on standard packs?

Product name, variety, origin (Karnataka, India), net weight, ingredients list, nutritional table, manufacturer name and address, best-before date, and batch number. Country-specific requirements (Halal marks, bar codes, specific nutrition formats) can be incorporated with advance notice.

Quality & Testing
How do you ensure quality before shipment?
  1. Source inspection at the mill or farm
  2. Sortex cleaning (rice) or grading (coconut)
  3. Moisture testing before packing
  4. Third-party lab analysis (NABL or SGS accredited) on request
  5. Pre-shipment physical inspection available on buyer appointment
Can I arrange a pre-shipment inspection?

Yes. You may appoint SGS, Intertek, Bureau Veritas, or any accredited agency to inspect goods at our warehouse before loading. Inspection costs are borne by the buyer. We fully cooperate and provide access, samples, and lab reports.

Are lab certificates available?

Yes. NABL-accredited and SGS lab reports are available for every commercial shipment on request. Reports typically cover moisture content, broken grain percentage, foreign matter, milling degree, and microbiological parameters where applicable.

After-Sales & Claims
What happens if there is a quality issue on arrival?

Notify us in writing within 5 days of arrival with a third-party (SGS or equivalent) lab report and photographic evidence. Where a deviation from agreed specification is substantiated, we offer a credit note, partial refund, or replacement in the next shipment. Unsubstantiated claims are not accepted.

What happens if the shipment is delayed at sea?

Delays due to vessel scheduling, port congestion, or customs are beyond our control once goods are handed to the carrier. We provide proactive tracking updates. For time-sensitive orders, we recommend building buffer time into your schedule.

Can I reorder without going through the sample process again?

Yes. Repeat orders are processed against the agreed specification from your first shipment. We send a brief specification re-confirmation before production and include a fresh lab report with the shipping documents.

Documentation & Compliance
What documents come with every shipment?
  • Commercial Invoice with full HS code details
  • Packing List itemised by carton and bag
  • Certificate of Origin (CoO) via APEDA or Chamber of Commerce
  • Phytosanitary Certificate from India’s Plant Quarantine Authority
  • Bill of Lading — OBL or Surrendered BL as required
  • Fumigation Certificate for wooden pallets where required

Additional certificates (Health Certificate, Halal, GSTP Form A, etc.) are available on advance request.

Do you provide Halal certification?

Our products are naturally Halal (plant-based, no animal-derived ingredients). A formal Halal certificate from an accredited body can be arranged on request with 2–3 weeks lead time at additional cost. This is commonly required for supermarket retail in GCC countries, Malaysia, and Indonesia.

Do you have organic certification?

We do not currently hold organic certification (USDA NOP, EU Organic, or India Organic). Organically grown Karnataka rice and coconut products can be sourced for large volume orders, but the certification process requires a 3–6 month lead time. Contact us to discuss your requirement.

What HS codes apply to your products?
  • Sona Masuri (milled rice): HS 1006.30
  • Brown rice: HS 1006.20
  • Desiccated coconut: HS 0801.11 / 0801.12
  • Coconut oil (crude): HS 1513.11
  • Coconut oil (refined): HS 1513.19

We include the correct HS code on every invoice. Your country may use specific sub-codes — verify with your customs broker.

Are you registered with APEDA?

Yes. Apex Origin Global is registered with APEDA (Agricultural and Processed Food Products Export Development Authority), the mandatory government body for Indian agricultural exporters. Our registration number is available on request and can be verified on the official APEDA portal.

Can you provide a Certificate of Origin for preferential duty rates?

Yes. For countries with preferential trade agreements with India (ASEAN-India FTA, SAFTA, AIFTA, India-UAE CEPA, India-Australia ECTA, etc.), we issue the appropriate Form A or GSP certificate of origin that may entitle your shipment to reduced import duties. Inform us of your destination country at the time of order.

Region-Specific Import Requirements
What is required to import into the European Union?

EU imports of rice and coconut products require: phytosanitary certificate, certificate of origin, commercial invoice with HS code, packing list, and bill of lading. For retail sale, EU nutritional labelling (Regulation 1169/2011) is mandatory. Food contact packaging must comply with EU Regulation 10/2011. We prepare EU-compliant packs on request.

EUGermanyNetherlandsFranceItalySpain
What is required to import into the United Kingdom?

UK imports require a UK Customs Entry (via CDS), commercial invoice, packing list, certificate of origin, and phytosanitary certificate. Indian rice and coconut products are subject to the UK Global Tariff (UKGT). Post-Brexit, UK labelling regulations differ from EU requirements. Recommend using a UK-licensed customs broker.

UK
What is required to import into the USA?

US food imports are regulated by the FDA. Requirements include: FDA prior notice (submitted before vessel arrival), importer of record registration, commercial invoice with HTS code (1006.30.9000 for milled rice), bill of lading, and packing list. The FDA may hold and test shipments from suppliers not previously in their database. Use a licensed US customs broker and a freight forwarder experienced with FDA-regulated products.

USACanada
What is required to import into GCC countries?

GCC imports of food products generally require: certificate of origin endorsed by the Indian Chamber of Commerce and APEDA, phytosanitary certificate, commercial invoice, packing list, bill of lading, and Arabic labelling on all retail packs. Saudi Arabia additionally requires a health certificate from FSSAI. Halal certification is strongly preferred for retail. We supply all standard GCC documents and apply Arabic/English dual labels for this market as standard.

UAESaudi ArabiaOmanQatarKuwaitBahrain
What is required to import into Australia or New Zealand?

Australian imports of plant products are regulated by DAFF (Department of Agriculture, Fisheries and Forestry). A phytosanitary certificate is mandatory. Rice may require fumigation prior to export. New Zealand has similar biosecurity requirements under MPI. Both countries may require import permits for certain product categories. Coordinate with an Australian or NZ customs broker before ordering.

AustraliaNew Zealand
Can you export to African markets?

Yes. We ship to East Africa (Kenya, Tanzania, Uganda, Ethiopia), West Africa (Nigeria, Ghana, Ivory Coast), and Southern Africa (South Africa, Mozambique, Zambia). Most African markets require a certificate of origin, phytosanitary certificate, commercial invoice, and packing list. Some countries require consularisation (embassy attestation of documents in India). We advise on a country-by-country basis.

What is required to import into Southeast Asian markets?

Requirements vary by country. Most ASEAN members (Singapore, Malaysia, Indonesia, Thailand, Philippines, Vietnam) require a phytosanitary certificate and certificate of origin. Under the ASEAN-India FTA, preferential duty rates may apply with Form AI. Singapore is a free port with minimal restrictions. Indonesia requires an import licence (SPI) for rice from the National Food Agency (Bulog). We advise in detail once you confirm your destination.

Shipping & Logistics
What is the difference between LCL and FCL?

LCL (Less than Container Load): your goods share a container with other exporters. Ideal for orders below 15–18 MT. Lower entry cost but slightly higher per-unit freight and longer transit due to consolidation at both ends.

FCL (Full Container Load): you fill the entire container. Available in 20ft (~24–26 MT of rice) and 40ft (~26–28 MT of rice). Lower per-unit freight for large orders and faster customs clearance.

What do FOB, CFR, and CIF mean?

FOB (Free On Board): we deliver to the Indian port and load the goods. You pay freight and insurance from that point onward and arrange your own shipping.

CFR (Cost and Freight): we pay ocean freight to your destination port. You arrange insurance and handle customs clearance at your end.

CIF (Cost, Insurance and Freight): we pay both freight and insurance to your destination port. You handle clearance and onward delivery. CIF is the most common choice for first-time buyers.

How long from order to shipment?

Standard lead time is 14–21 days from confirmed purchase order and advance payment (or LC). This includes: sourcing and quality check (3–5 days), packing and labelling (5–7 days), port delivery and loading (3–5 days), and documentation (2–3 days, running in parallel).

Private label orders with new packaging require an additional 3–4 weeks for artwork approval and packaging production.

Do you handle freight booking and forwarding?

Yes. We work with established freight forwarding partners across all major Indian ports. Under CFR or CIF terms we manage ocean freight booking on your behalf. If you prefer your own freight forwarder, we coordinate with them directly for loading instructions and documentation.

Is cargo insurance included?

Cargo insurance is included under CIF terms only. Under FOB and CFR, insurance is the buyer’s responsibility. We can arrange marine cargo insurance at competitive rates for any shipment if required — just let us know at the quotation stage.
